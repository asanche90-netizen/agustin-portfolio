Background

I came to graphic design through a BFA in Painting from Kent State and years managing bars and restaurants. Both taught me things design school doesn't: how to read a room, how to make something with your hands until it's right, how to serve people without losing your perspective.

Now I work across brand identity, packaging, editorial, environmental design, and motion. I'm finishing an AAS in Graphic Design at Seattle Central College in June 2025, and I run Agustin Sanchez Studio for freelance work on the side.

Approach

A painter's training gives you a different relationship to composition than someone who learned design first. You think about weight, about where the eye goes and why, about what the surface is actually doing. I bring that into every layout and identity system I build.

The hospitality background means I think about the person on the other end of whatever I'm making. A brand is a set of promises. A package is a first impression. A report is someone's case to an audience that has to trust them. Design that forgets those stakes tends to show it.

Currently

Based in Ballard, Seattle. Available for full-time roles and select freelance. I'm most interested in brands that have something real to say — food and beverage, culture, civic, environmental.
